About

Securing connected physical infrastructure.

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Virginia Commonwealth University, with more than ten years of research experience in cybersecurity, IoT security, machine learning, and cyber-physical systems.

My work sits at the hardware-software boundary. I use side-channel sensing, such as power and electromagnetic emissions, together with data-driven methods to monitor and protect physical processes. The main application domain is additive manufacturing and 3D bioprinting, where I study both how these systems can be attacked and how to detect those attacks in real time. The goal is trustworthy, resilient cyber-physical manufacturing.

I care about teaching as much as research, with interests across cybersecurity education, digital forensics, and embedded systems.
